I told myself I'd just buy one decent keyboard and stop. That was four keyboards ago.

## How it started

My membrane keyboard started double-registering keystrokes. I figured I'd spend around $50 on something better. Then I fell into a YouTube rabbit hole and learned that mechanical keyboards are a whole hobby with custom builds, group buys, and frankly unhinged spending.

## The trap

The hobby is designed to make you spend more:

- Entry-level boards feel noticeably better than membrane
- But you can *always* feel the gap to the next tier
- Group buys mean you pay now and wait 12 months for a board

## How to not go broke

**Start with a hot-swap board.** Don't solder anything until you know what switch feel you actually like. The Keychron V series is the classic recommendation for good reason.

**Buy switches in small testers first.** I wasted money on full packs of switches I ended up not liking.

**Stay off r/mechanicalkeyboards.** Genuinely. Every post is either a flex or an enabler.

## My current setup

I landed on a 65% layout with tactile switches. It hits the balance between compact and functional — I don't miss the numpad, and function keys are a layer away.

The rabbit hole is real. But if you set a hard budget and stick to it, you come out the other side with a keyboard you actually enjoy using every day.
